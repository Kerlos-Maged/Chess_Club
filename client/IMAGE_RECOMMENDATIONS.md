# Image Placement & Recommendations for Chess Club Website

## Recommended Images to Source/Add

### 1. **Hero Section Banner**
- **Type**: Large high-quality image (1920x600px)
- **Content**: Chess game in progress, OR students playing chess, OR school chess tournament
- **Location**: Background or right side of hero
- **Recommendation**: Action shot showing dynamic chess gameplay

### 2. **Programs Section** (3 images)
- **Type**: Square images (400x400px each)
- **Content**: 
  - Beginner Training: Young students learning chess basics
  - Advanced Training: Focused players in competitive match
  - Community: Group of students together/social gathering
- **Recommendation**: Real photos from school chess club activities

### 3. **School Background Image**
- **Type**: School building or classroom (1920x1080px)
- **Content**: School campus, classroom interior, or auditorium
- **Usage**: Subtle background with opacity in strategic sections
- **Recommendation**: Your actual school building or chess hall

### 4. **Live Matches Section**
- **Type**: Wide banner (1920x400px)
- **Content**: Chess board close-up, tournament setup, or viewing area
- **Recommendation**: Professional chess match or tournament environment

### 5. **Team/Coaches Section** (New)
- **Type**: Profile images (200x200px circular)
- **Content**: Coach/advisor headshots, club leader photos
- **Recommendation**: Professional headshots of club members

### 6. **Stats Background**
- **Type**: Decorative background (1920x600px)
- **Content**: School hallway, students in school, or chess-themed pattern
- **Usage**: Semi-transparent overlay with statistics

### 7. **Testimonials Section**
- **Type**: Student photos (150x150px circular)
- **Content**: Student headshots or casual photos
- **Recommendation**: Real club members speaking

## Implementation Plan

All image placeholders are added with:
- Clear "Image Placeholder" sections
- Recommended dimensions
- Suggested content descriptions
- Easy CSS classes to replace with actual images

Replace placeholder divs with `<img>` tags when images are ready.
